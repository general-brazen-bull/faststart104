The Story Behind The Legend

The Origin of FAST START 104

In the 1940s and 50s, George Hartley Jr. of Jasper, Alberta, was carrying out distillation experiments in tunnels he had dug beneath the town. Working with a custom-built still, his focus was simple: produce the purest alcohol possible.

He came from a long line of distillers. His father, George Hartley Sr., was the first Presbyterian pastor in British Columbia and, at the same time, a moonshiner. The family had left the United States during Prohibition, looking for more freedom and a quieter life in Canada.

Hartley Sr. was known for his obsession with juniper. Hartley Jr. took a different direction and became focused on purity. Over time, he refined a process that produced high-potency, exceptionally clean alcohol.

That alcohol had another property. It was highly flammable.

At some point, he began experimenting with it as a fuel. He converted an early-1930s Dodge pickup to run on alcohol. Around town, he was known for demonstrating that the truck could run on water. What he was pouring into the tank looked like water, but it was his distilled alcohol.

Jasper winters being what they are, getting an engine started was not always straightforward. Hartley carried a small bottle with him to prime the carburetor when needed.

That bottle is the origin of FAST START 104.

Alongside it, he developed a higher-proof neutral spirit, now produced as Proof Beyond. FAST START 104 came out of the same work, a slightly less volatile product that was easy to drink at a high alcohol level and practical to carry.

Production and Disappearance

Small-scale production continued within the family through the 1960s. The packaging from that period set the tone for what you see today: functional, direct, and built to communicate purpose without explanation.

At some point, production stopped.

The reasons were never formally documented. What remained were the formulas, the equipment, and the knowledge, passed quietly through the family.

For a number of years, FAST START 104 was simply not available.

Return to Production

The rights to the formulas and methods remained within the Hartley family. After years of discussion and no clear direction, the decision has now been made to produce them again.

FAST START 104 is based on that original work.

The current packaging reflects both the original design language and the period in which it was last produced. It is not a recreation. It is a continuation.

Jasper and Local History

George Hartley Jr. is widely recognized as the basis for the character Fraser McKillop in John McLay's On Mountaintop Rock (2001). The setting, the vehicle, and the personality align closely with how he was known in Jasper.

A 1930s Dodge pickup, weathered and practical, was exactly the kind of vehicle associated with him. It remains part of the local history.

What Remains

There are still aspects of Hartley's work and life that are not fully understood, even within the family. What is clear is the outcome: a set of formulations built around purity, strength, and practicality.

FAST START 104 carries that forward.
